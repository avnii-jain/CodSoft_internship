Albert Einstein

March 14, 1879 - April 18, 1955

About the Legend

  • Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879 and raised in a secular Jewish family.
  • His immense contributions to the field of physics and his theory of relativity which fundamentally changed our understanding of the universe.
  • He was a German-born physicist who developed the special and general theories of relativity and won the Nobel Prize for Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
  • Einstein's fame and the great success of his theories created a backlash.
  • Einstein's brain had unusually well-developed prefrontal cortex and parietal lobes, which are associated with abstract thinking, mathematical skills, and spatial reasoning.
  • Einstein developed a theory of special and general relativity, which helped to complicate and expand upon theories.
  • Albert Einstein had a massive influence on contemporary physics. His theory of relativity shifted contemporary understanding of space completely.
  • Einstein's fame grew after Arthur Eddington presented the results of his expedition to the Royal Society in 1919.
  • Einstein's political activity as a Zionist activist may have also contributed to his iconic status.
  • His theories of relativity are considered to be the foundation of modern astronomy and astrophysics.
  • Einstein was an influential humanist who spoke widely about politics, ethics, and social causes. His theories were instrumental in shaping the atomic age.
  • Einstein's legacy continues to influence scientists today. His theories continue to be used to explain the workings of the universe.
